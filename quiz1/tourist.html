Tourist Destination

1. Istana Maimun


Istana Maimun is the Deli Sultanate palace in Deli Land for various Deli Malays with Deli Malays which is one of the icons of Medan City, North Sumatra located on Brigadir Jenderal Katamso Street, Aur, Medan Maimun, Medan.

Designed by architect Capt. Theodoor van Erp, a Dutch soldier who was built on the orders of the Sultan of Deli, Sultan Ma'moen Al Rasyid. Construction of this palace began on August 26, 1888 and was completed on May 18, 1891. Maimun Palace has an area of ​​2,772 m2 and 30 rooms. Maimun Palace consists of 2 floors and has 3 parts, consists of the main building, the left wing building and the right wing building. This palace building faces east and on the front side there is the Al-Mashun Mosque building or better known as the Masjid Raya Medan.

Istana Maimun is a tourist destination not only because of its old age, but also its unique interior design, combining elements of Deli Malay cultural heritage, with Islamic, Spanish, Indian, Dutch and Italian styles. But unfortunately, this tourist spot is not free from the street vendors area

2. Museum Tjong A Fie Mansion


Museum Tjong A Fie Mansion is a mansion in Medan City which was built by Tjong A Fie, a successful merchant in the city of Medan in the late 19th to early 20th centuries and became one of the most influential figures in this city. Currently the mansion has been converted into a museum, and you can come and admire its Art Deco-style building with a combination of Chinese and European cultural influences.

It is said that the architecture was influenced by the famous Cheong Fatt Tze Mansion in Penang, which appeared in one of the climactic scenes of the film Crazy Rich Asians!

3. Masjid Raya Medan


Masjid Raya Medan or Masjid Raya Al-Mashun is a mosque located in the city of Medan, Indonesia. This mosque was built in 1906 and completed in 1909. At the beginning of its establishment, the mosque was integrated with the palace complex. The architectural style is typical of the Middle East, India and Spain. This mosque is octagonal in shape and has wings on the south, east, north, and west. Masjid Raya Medan is a witness to the history of the Deli Malay civilization, which has a close relationship with the Deli Sultanate.

Sultan Ma'mun Al Rasyid Perkasa Alam as the leader of the Deli Sultanate started the construction of the Al Mashun Grand Mosque on August 21, 1906 (1 Rajab 1324 Hijriah). The entire construction was completed on September 10, 1909 (25 Shaban 1329 Hijri) as well as being used which was marked by the implementation of the first Friday prayer at this mosque. The entire construction cost one million Guilders. The Sultan deliberately built this royal mosque magnificently, because in principle it was more important than the splendor of his own palace, Istana Maimun. The funding for the construction of this mosque was borne by the Sultan himself, but it is said that Tjong A Fie, a Medan city figure from Chinese ethnicity who was contemporaries of Sultan Ma'moen Al Rasyid contributed to funding the construction of this mosque.

4. Tjong Yong Hian Gallery


Tjong Yong Hian Gallery is a garden that was built by Tjong Yong Hian, one of the successful businessmen and other influential figures in Medan City in the late 19th century. He is also the older brother of Tjong A Fie, the founder of the Museum Tjong A Fie Mansion - the two worked together to build business to become one of the two richest men in Medan.

The garden, also known as the Flower Garden Garden or Tjong Yong Hian Park, was inaugurated in 2011, and was formerly the home of Tjong Yong Hian. This park was opened as one of the tourist attractions in Medan, to commemorate Tjong Yong Hian's great role in the development of the city.

5. Maha Vihara Maitreya


Maha Vihara Maitreya, is a Buddhist temple located on Medan, which is claimed to be the largest non-historical buddhist temple in Indonesia. This temple is often called Vihara Cemara Asri because it is located in housing complex of Cemara Asri. Maha Vihara Maitreya was built in 1991 on an area of 4.5 hectares and was inaugurated on August 21, 2008.

The Temple building is divided into three main rooms. The first building there is a common Baktisala that a place of worship Buddha Sakyamuni, Bodhisattva and the Bodhisattva Satyakalama Avolokitesvara. The building has a capacity of 1,500 people. In the second building of the Maitreya Baktisala area with a capacity of 2,500 people. In this section there are also has Baktisala Holy Patriarch. In addition, there is a banquet hall as a special dining room reception. Meanwhile, the third building is a meeting hall with a capacity of 2,000 people. All the buildings has guest house, only guesthouse in one building has the most complete facilities, namely the existence of office space, meeting rooms, recording studios and a communal kitchen.
